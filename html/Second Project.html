Sony PlayStation 4 Pro review

PS4 is back with it's pro version. It's beautiful, it's Sharp... it's 4K!

Closing in on 50 million units sold, it's safe to say Sony's PlayStation 4 has been a huge success, significantly outpacing its closest competition, Microsoft's Xbox One. But just three years since its debut, Sony has released a hardware step-up model of the PS4, aptly called the PlayStation 4 Pro. As you can guess by the name, this isn't the PlayStation 5. It's fully compatible with all of the existing games, apps and nearly all the PS4 accessories currently on the market (or in your personal collection). The new model hit stores on November 10 for $399 in the US, AU$559 in Australia and 38,990 Rupees in India.

SONY PLAYSTATION 4 PRO DESIGN: PRO EVO

There's not much difference between the PS4 Pro and the original PS4. As the difference lies in size and some of its improvement as it's few centimeters bigger in every direction. It's similar around the back with the same HDMI, Ethernet, digital optical audio and playstation camera ports.The HDMI ports does get a boost to 4K.

CONTROLLER

DUALSHOCK 4

The DualShock 4 is the best controller Sony has ever made. It's better than DualShock 3 and the controller is little bit heavier and is little bit bigger. The DualShock 4 is appealing not just to the eye but, more importantly, to the hands.

  • BUTTONS

Buttons are buttery smooth and feels great while playing, Share and option buttons are too far away. As the share button lets players to record and upload videos and screenshots to the web.

  • TOUCHPAD

The Toucpad is improved and is placed on the front of the controller and we did discover that it acts like a button itself and is a clickable 2-point touchpad with a resolution of 1920x900. The functionality of the touchpad will differ from between games.you are able to zoom, swipe, pinch, and scroll with the intuitive touchpad.

  • GRIP

The quality of the DualShock 4 is great as the grip can be seen on both the back of the controller and on the analog sticks. The texture and polishing is nice and is comfortable while playing games.

  • ANALOG STICKS

Sony has replaced the DualShock 3's inwardly-curving triggers with triggers that curve outward. Analog sticks are more better and are improved.

  • BATTERY

The DualShock 4 comes with a lithium-ion rechargeable battery and charges using a micro USB, but many reviews note the DualShock 4 has a battery life of roughly 7-8 hours. The DualShock 4 battery isn't intended to be replaced.

Conclusion:

The DualShock 4 is the best controller Sony has ever made.

OPERATING SYSTEM

USER INTERFACE & EXPERIENCE

The big news here is that unlike the Microsoft Xbox One S, the PS4 Pro doesn't have a 4K Blu-ray drive - which obviously means you won't be able to watch UHD Blu-rays on it. It'll still play 1080p discs, but stick in a 4K Blu-ray and it'll just comes up as "Unsupported" on the home screen. Sony's reasoning for omitting one? The world's moved on; streaming services such as Netflix are more popular than buying discs. It's a fair point, but given Microsoft managed to squeeze one into the One S, it's still a disappointment that the more expensive PS4 Pro doesn't have one too. You do get 4K video, though, through the usual suspects - or at least you should do by launch. Netflix is already running in 4K, and YouTube will get there by launch too, but there's no HDR streaming on Netflix or Amazon just yet. This is kind of a moot point for most 4K TV owners, though. Their TVs should already have UHD-ready Amazon, Netflix and YouTube apps, just like our Samsung and Philips test screens do. The upshot is that while The PS4 Pro may offer the best console gaming experience, it doesn't offer quite the best home entertainment experience; you'll need another box for that.

Conclusion:

After seven or eight years of the Xbox 360 and PS3, navigating the PS4's menus and UI quickly and with little lag is one of the more refreshing elements of the next-gen console experience.

GAMES

  • A Unified Gaming Community

With up to 4K streaming and 4K auto-upscaling for video content.

Have you somehow managed to buy all 700+ PS4 games released so far? No, thought not. But whatever is currently in your collection will play nicely on PS4 Pro. It'll upscale all of those games to 4K resolution, too. The real difference comes with games that have been optimised for PS4 Pro.

It's one major advantage that the PS4 Pro has over the Xbox One S. Because the Pro has more power as opposed to just gaining 4K capabilities as the Xbox did, those fully optimised games will look better than anything you can play on Microsoft's console. The One S does have a handful of its own 'optimised' games, but these are optimised only for HDR rather than performance. Developers can choose how they take advantage of the improved performance, either bumping the resolution, increasing frame rates, adding more detailed textures, or a combination of all three.

The emphasis on powerful hardware dedicated to gaming software is already yielding tangible advantages over the competition, with games like Battlefield 4 running in higher resolution on the PlayStation 4 than the Xbox One. However, at launch, the PS4 has failed to muster a software library that sells that hardware.

Conclusion:

Make no mistake: the PS4 is demonstrably more powerful than the competition.

OTHER EXCLUSIVE ACCESSORIES & FEATURES

The PS4 Pro's heftier internals also give PlayStation VR a boost. Developers can again choose how to use that power, either bumping resolution, adding more effects, or improving the anti-aliasing. In theory, these improvements will make your games more immersive, but it's tricky when you can't compare them side-by-side. The PSVR's display isn't getting any extra pixels, either.

The biggest problem with PSVR? Its processing box doesn't support 4K or HDR, so you have to manually swap over the cables every time you switch between VR headset and TV. It feels anything but "Pro".

The PS4 Pro will launch with Netflix support at 4K resolution in addition to a YouTube app with 4K and HDR compatibility. More apps will open up support for 4K and HDR features as the platform matures. Also exclusive to the PS4 Pro is improved bandwidth for the Remote Play and Share Play options, which let you stream gameplay over the internet. Both modes will be able to share, stream and play at 1080p, which is a bump up from the standard PS4's 720p cap

Conclusion:

Some of it are perfect & some of it are not.

VERDICT

NEXT GEN IS HERE!

The PS4 hints at plenty of other possibilities. Local network play via the PS Vita has an enormous amount of potential. The PlayStation App and even the Playstation Camera may provide opportunities for developers to broaden the appeal of the PlayStation 4 beyond the hardcore audience it currently seems so intent on courting. Unlike the PlayStation 3, Sony's latest effort was built to evolve.

But the PlayStation 4's focus on gaming - and only gaming - is undermined by a distinct lack of compelling software. That failing is sure to improve - better games and more of them will appear on the PlayStation 4 - but right now, this is a game console without a game to recommend it. Early adopters of the PS4 this fall are buying potential energy. We're just waiting for a place to spend it.

Let's face it - PS4 Pro is a beast.

The Good- The PS4 Pro outputs a 4k-HDR signal that's compatible with high-end TVs, and promises better visuals and increased frame rates for certain games. The console includes a 1TB hard drive, and kept works with all ps4 games, apps and accessories to date.

The Bad- Out of the gate, only a handful of titles offer dramatic improvements. In reality, most games that support Pro enhancements don't offer a drastic difference in quality when comparing standard and Pro games side-by-side. Specific information on game improvements can be shoddy. The PS4 Pro does not play Ultra HD 4K Blu-ray discs

The Bottom- Line The PlayStation 4 Pro only shows noticeable improvements in a limited number of the games we were able to test, but that could change with titles coming in the months and years ahead.

Ultimately, the PS4 Pro is a system for console gamers who have a 4K TV and want the best gaming experience possible. If you meet these criteria, it's a must-buy.
